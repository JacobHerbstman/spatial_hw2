\begin{abstract}
This short paper studies how a productivity shock to cognitive non-routine work affects
spatial adjustment in a simplified dynamic trade-and-migration model of U.S. states. I use
a four-sector version of the framework in \citet{caliendo2019trade} and scale state-sector
productivity shocks with a cognitive-intensity matrix built from O*NET, OEWS, and QCEW
data. The quantitative exercise compares an immediate AI shock with an anticipated shock
that starts later. The model is solved in a MATLAB-faithful reference mode, and the reported
results come from the largest common shock size that converges in both timing scenarios.
\end{abstract}
