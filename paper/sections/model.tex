\section{Model}

The model is a simplified dynamic spatial equilibrium over U.S. states and four sectors:
manufacturing, construction, wholesale/retail, and services. As in \citet{caliendo2019trade},
workers choose across location-sector labor markets subject to migration frictions, goods are
traded with sectoral trade structure, and the model is solved in relative time differences.

The quantitative implementation keeps the baseline and counterfactual recursion close to the
MATLAB replication logic. In particular, the reference results reported here use a
MATLAB-faithful outer-loop configuration with no Anderson acceleration, no warm-started static
guesses, no threading, and a fixed $0.5$ relaxation update for the value-difference path.

The policy object is a productivity shifter $\lambda_{jnt}$ applied to state-sector cells. The
baseline path is unchanged, and the counterfactual changes only these productivity terms. The
immediate scenario activates the AI shock at $t=1$, while the anticipated scenario delays
activation until $t=21$.
